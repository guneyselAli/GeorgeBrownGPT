
Program Name: Child Development Practitioner (ECE Apprenticeship)
Program Code: C602
Method of Study: Part-time
Duration: 2.5 – 3 Years
Tuition: $70 for classes & $20 for placements
Centre: Community Services & Early Childhood
School: Early Childhood
Location: George Brown College at Toronto Metropolitan University
Starting Months: September, January, May
Winter 2022 Delivery: Child Development Practitioner classes that start January 2023 will be delivered in a hybrid model with 80% delivered online and 20% delivered in-person. 
This program is designed for people already working in the field of Early Childhood who wish to pursue formal studies and receive certification as a Child Development Practitioner (CDP). CDP apprentices are required to take 23 modules of in-school training. This training is completed part-time (night classes) and is currently being offered in a hybrid (both in-person and online) format.
This apprenticeship program is delivered by the Ministry of Labour, Immigration, Training and Skills Development (MLITSD) through George Brown College’s School of Early Childhood.
This course will also enhance participant's awareness of community resources available to support them.
Should students/apprentices wish to complete their diploma in Early Childhood Education, they will be required to complete three General Education subjects, including College English, and Introduction Sociology.
Students will complete six placements with various age-groups, five of which will be completed at their place of employment. The final placement (Advanced Placement) will be three weeks long (full-time) and must be completed at a George Brown College Lab School.
