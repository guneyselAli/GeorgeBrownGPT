Full Description
IMPORTANT NOTE: This program is currently being delivered in a hybrid format that includes both in-person and online instruction.
George Brown College’s English as a Second Language (ESL) (Part-Time) certificate
is a non-credit program that will help you develop the English language skills you need to succeed in a fast-paced transformational global environment.
With eight levels of instruction that range from low beginner to advanced, and a variety of elective courses to suit your personal, professional, and academic learning needs, you will build confidence in your skills as you progress through the program.
Courses you complete in this ESL program are recognized by our full-time English for Academic Purposes (EAP) program. The more ESL courses you complete, the fewer EAP courses you will be required to take to complete that certificate. Click on the ‘Career & Postgraduate Study Opportunities’ tab located on the left side of this page for more details.
PLEASE NOTE:
These ESL courses do not count as credit toward other George Brown programs.

Admission Requirements
If you are new to the program and it is your first time applying, you must take the level placement test. You will start the program from the level based on the placement test results.
Alternatively, if you have completed an ESL course at George Brown College within the last six months, you can apply to start at the next level without having to complete a placement test.
Special Requirements
You must achieve a grade of B (73%) or better to successfully complete each course.
There are two requirements for completing the certificate:
Complete at least five (5) ESL courses at GBC;
Complete
ESL 9068 (ESL IIIB ADVANCED)
.
Example:
If you take the level placement test and begin with
ESL 9000 (ESL IA LOW BEGINNER)
, you will need to complete all 8 compulsory courses (Therefore, completed at least 5 ESL courses and completed
ESL 9068 (ESL IIIB ADVANCED)
to achieve the certificate.
If you take the level placement test and begin with
ESL 9066 (ESL IIC HIGH INTERMEDIATE)
, you will need to complete
ESL 9066 (ESL IIC HIGH INTERMEDIATE)
,
ESL 9067 (IIIA LOW ADVANCED)
and
ESL 9068 (ESL IIIB ADVANCED)
plus 2 elective courses (Therefore, completed at least 5 ESL courses and completed
ESL 9068 (ESL IIIB ADVANCED)
) to achieve the certificate.
